# When to Research vs Summarize

Choose the lightest useful mode.

## Summarize / synthesize only
Use when:
- the capture is personal reflection or journaling
- the answer mainly requires organizing the user's own thought
- external facts will not change the recommendation much
- the user appears to want clarity, not validation

## Lightweight research
Use when:
- a few external facts could materially sharpen the answer
- the question is market-facing or technical but narrow
- there is a clear concept to validate quickly

## Deeper research
Use when:
- the result may influence product direction, pricing, technical architecture, or market choice
- the user is comparing alternatives
- important risks depend on external information
- a recommendation would be weak without citations

## Escalation rule

If a confident recommendation would require facts you do not have, research.
If the user's note is too vague to research well, first sharpen the question.

## Avoid over-researching

Do not browse just to make the answer look smart.
Only research facts that change the decision.
