# ICP Scorecard

Score each candidate ICP from 1-5 on every dimension.

## Dimensions

### 1. Pain intensity
How painful is the current workflow?
- 1 = mild inconvenience
- 3 = annoying repeated friction
- 5 = painful, expensive, or embarrassing to do badly

### 2. Frequency
How often does the problem occur?
- 1 = monthly or rarer
- 3 = weekly
- 5 = daily or many times per day

### 3. Speed-to-value
How quickly can ClawMeld prove value?
- 1 = value emerges after weeks
- 3 = value within a few days
- 5 = value visible in first session

### 4. Willingness to pay
Is there a believable budget?
- 1 = hobbyist / low willingness
- 3 = prosumer / moderate willingness
- 5 = business-critical / direct ROI

### 5. Distribution access
Can Joel realistically reach this audience?
- 1 = no obvious channel
- 3 = weak but plausible channel
- 5 = direct reach, communities, or clear niche distribution

### 6. Differentiation fit
Does ClawMeld have a real edge here?
- 1 = commodity feature set
- 3 = some product angle
- 5 = uniquely strong workflow or insight loop

## Suggested interpretation

- `24-30`: strong candidate; prioritize testing
- `18-23`: promising but needs sharper positioning
- `12-17`: weak; only pursue with special evidence
- `<12`: kill or park

## Candidate ICP examples

- founders capturing ideas while driving/walking
- creators turning voice thoughts into publishable content
- consultants turning field notes into client-ready deliverables
- sales reps turning voice notes into CRM updates
- executives capturing follow-ups after meetings

## Output recommendation

Return:
- top ICP
- runner-up ICP
- why the winner is better
- one risk that could invalidate the choice
