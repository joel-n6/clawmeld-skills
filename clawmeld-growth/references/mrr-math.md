# MRR Math

Use simple, explicit math.

## Core formulas

- `MRR = paying_customers * monthly_price`
- `Customers needed = target_MRR / monthly_price`
- `Trial-to-paid customers needed = paying_customers / conversion_rate`
- `Leads needed = trial_signups / lead_to_trial_rate`

## Quick targets for $10k/month

### B2C / prosumer
- `$10/mo` -> `1000` paying users
- `$15/mo` -> `667` paying users
- `$20/mo` -> `500` paying users
- `$30/mo` -> `334` paying users

### Annual plans
Approximate annual equivalent:
- `$99/yr` -> ~`$8.25 MRR` per active subscriber -> ~`1213` active subs
- `$199/yr` -> ~`$16.58 MRR` per active subscriber -> ~`603` active subs

### B2B-lite
- `$49/mo` -> `205` accounts
- `$99/mo` -> `102` accounts
- `$249/mo` -> `41` accounts
- `$499/mo` -> `21` accounts

## Heuristics

- If the product is consumer-like and priced under `$15/mo`, distribution must be strong.
- If the workflow saves money or time for a business user, test higher pricing early.
- If retention is weak, acquisition math is fantasy.
- If willingness to pay is unclear, validate with manual service before building more software.

## Minimum numbers to ask for

When possible, estimate:
- price tested
- activated users
- weekly retained users
- conversion to paid
- likely acquisition channel

## Red flags

- needing 1000+ users without a real channel
- pricing based on competitor parity instead of value
- "we'll figure out monetization later"
- low-frequency use case with monthly subscription pricing
